## Opening Procedures

### 1. Phone System Login & Setup
ðŸ“ž *Daily Task â€” First Thing*

Logging into the phone system enables call tracking, routing from call centers, and after-hours message retrieval.

#### Step 1: Access Phone System
- Arrive at designated time before clinic opens
- Power on phone/computer system if needed
- Log in with your unique credentials
- Verify your extension is active

#### Step 2: Check Voicemail & After-Hours Messages
- Access voicemail system
- Listen to all messages from after-hours/call center
- Document urgent messages for immediate follow-up
- Note patient callbacks needed

#### Step 3: Verify Phone Routing
- Confirm calls are routing to front desk correctly
- Test internal extension transfers
- Verify after-hours routing is disabled (if applicable)
- Check that hold music/messages are working

#### Step 4: Prepare for Incoming Calls
- Have scheduling system open and ready
- Keep patient lookup screen accessible
- Have notepad ready for messages
- Review any special instructions for the day

> **Why This Matters:** Proper phone login ensures call tracking for quality metrics, enables call center transfers, and captures after-hours activity.

### 2. Cash Drawer Opening
ðŸ’° *Daily Task â€” Before First Patient*

Proper cash drawer setup ensures accurate financial tracking throughout the day.

#### Step 1: Retrieve Cash Drawer
- Retrieve drawer from safe/secure location
- Use two-person verification if required by policy
- Sign out drawer on custody log

#### Step 2: Count Starting Cash
- Count all bills by denomination (largest to smallest)
- Count all coins
- Verify starting amount matches expected float (typically $100-200)
- Document any discrepancies immediately

#### Step 3: Document Opening
- Record starting amount on daily cash log
- Note date, time, and your initials
- Have witness sign if required
- Secure log in designated location

#### Step 4: Organize Drawer
- Arrange bills by denomination (largest in back)
- Ensure adequate change (ones, fives, quarters)
- Place checks/credit card receipts section ready
- Position drawer securely at workstation

> **Key Rule:** Never leave drawer unattended when unlocked. Lock when stepping away, even briefly.

### 3. Pre-Scrubbing the Schedule
ðŸ“‹ *Daily Task â€” Before Clinic Opens (or Day Prior)*

Pre-scrubbing reduces day-of surprises and ensures smooth patient flow.

#### Step 1: Pull Tomorrow's (or Today's) Schedule
- Print or open electronic schedule
- Review all appointments for the session
- Note appointment types and durations
- Identify new patients vs. established patients

#### Step 2: Verify Insurance Eligibility
- Check eligibility status for each patient
- Flag any patients not yet verified
- Run eligibility for flagged patients
- Document verification results

#### Step 3: Check for Missing Information
- Demographics complete? (address, phone, email)
- Insurance cards on file?
- Required forms signed/current?
- Outstanding balances noted?

#### Step 4: Identify Special Needs
- Interpreter needed?
- Wheelchair/accessibility requirements?
- Complex medical history to alert provider?
- Behavioral flags or special instructions?

#### Step 5: Prepare Patient Materials
- Pull any paper charts if used
- Queue electronic forms for completion
- Prepare new patient packets
- Print any needed requisitions

#### Step 6: Document & Communicate
- Create checklist of issues to address
- Flag problem appointments in system
- Communicate concerns to clinical team
- Note patients who may need extra time

> **Best Practice:** Pre-scrub 48-72 hours ahead when possible. This allows time to contact patients about missing information.

## Scheduling & Registration

### 4. New Patient Registration & Scheduling
ðŸ“ž *Applies to: New Patients Only*

Complete phone-based workflow for registering and scheduling new patients.

#### Step 1: Determine if Patient is New or Existing
- Ask: "Have you been seen at [Clinic Name] before?"
- If no or unsure, proceed to search system
- If yes, transfer to existing patient workflow

#### Step 2: Search System to Prevent Duplicates
> âš ï¸ **CRITICAL:** Always search before creating new record

- Search by: Last Name, First Name, Date of Birth
- Check spelling variations (Smith vs. Smyth, Jon vs. John)
- Check maiden/previous married names
- Search by phone number if unclear
- If found: Use existing record
- If NOT found: Proceed with registration

#### Step 3: Collect Patient Demographics
- Legal full name (First, Middle, Last) â€” ask them to spell it
- Date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY)
- Social Security Number (optional but helpful for billing)
- Current address (street, city, state, ZIP)
- Primary phone number
- Secondary phone number
- Email address
- Emergency contact (name, relationship, phone)
- Preferred language
- Communication preferences

#### Step 4: Collect Insurance Information
- Ask: "Do you have health insurance?"
- If yes:
  - Insurance company name
  - Member/Subscriber ID
  - Group number
  - Subscriber name and DOB (if different from patient)
  - Subscriber relationship to patient
- Inform: "Please bring your insurance card and photo ID to your appointment"
- If no insurance: Note as self-pay, discuss payment expectations

#### Step 5: Run Quick Eligibility Check
- Verify insurance is accepted by clinic
- Confirm plan is active
- Check if patient is covered under this plan
- If not accepted/active: Inform patient of self-pay status
- Document verification in system
- Set reminder for full verification 48-72 hours before visit

#### Step 6: Determine Appointment Type & Schedule
- Ask: "What is the reason for your visit?"
- Determine appropriate appointment type and length
- Ask about provider preference
- Check schedule for new patient slots (typically 30-60 min)
- Offer 2-3 available options
- Book with type marked as "New Patient"

#### Step 7: Confirm Appointment & Provide Instructions
- Confirm: "[Day], [Date] at [Time] with [Provider Name]"
- Provide: Clinic address, parking instructions
- Instruct: "Please arrive 10-15 minutes early"
- What to bring: Photo ID, insurance card(s), medication list, copay
- If applicable: Explain pre-visit electronic forms
- If copay known: State amount due at check-in

#### Step 8: Set Up Reminders & Pre-Visit Forms
- Enter appointment in system
- Set automatic reminders per clinic protocol
- Confirm preferred contact method
- Mention cancellation policy (24-hour notice)
- Send portal invitation/forms link if applicable

#### Step 9: Document Registration
- Create new patient record immediately
- Enter all demographics
- Enter insurance information
- Flag as "New Patient"
- Set reminder for full insurance verification
- Document registration date and registrar
- Add special notes (accessibility, interpreter, etc.)

ðŸ”„ *Applies to: Existing Patients*

Managing follow-up appointments and recall systems.

#### Step 5: Recall Management
- Review recall list weekly
- Contact patients due for routine care
- Use phone, mail, or portal messaging
- Document all outreach attempts
- Update status once appointment scheduled
- Follow clinic policy for contact attempts (typically 3)

### 7. Appointment Reminder Calls
ðŸ“± *Daily Task â€” 48-72 Hours Before Appointments*

Proactive reminders reduce no-shows and allow time for rescheduling.

#### Step 1: Generate Reminder List
- Pull list of appointments for 2-3 days out
- Prioritize: New patients, procedures, historically no-show patients
- Note any special prep instructions to relay

#### Step 2: Make Reminder Calls
Use script:
> "Hello, this is [Name] calling from [Clinic Name] to remind you of your appointment on [Day], [Date] at [Time] with [Provider]. Please arrive 10-15 minutes early. If you need to reschedule, please call us at [number]. Thank you!"

- Leave voicemail if no answer
- Note call outcome in system

#### Step 3: Handle Rescheduling Requests
- If patient needs to reschedule, do so during the call
- Offer 2-3 alternative times
- Update system immediately
- Send new confirmation if applicable

#### Step 4: Document Outcomes
- Confirmed
- Left voicemail
- Rescheduled (note new date/time)
- Unable to reach (wrong number, disconnected)
- Cancelled (note reason if given)

> **Tip:** Track patients who consistently don't answer â€” they may have updated contact info or prefer text/email reminders.

### 8. Patient Outreach
ðŸ“£ *Ongoing Task â€” Health Maintenance & Recalls*

Proactive outreach for preventive care and chronic disease management.

#### Step 1: Identify Outreach Needs
- Health maintenance due (annual physicals, screenings)
- Chronic care follow-ups overdue
- Lab/test results requiring follow-up visit
- Immunizations due
- Referral follow-ups not completed

#### Step 2: Generate Outreach Lists
- Run reports for overdue health maintenance
- Filter by date range and care type
- Prioritize high-risk or significantly overdue patients
- Remove recently scheduled patients

#### Step 3: Contact Patients
- Call during business hours (or per patient preference)
- Use appropriate script:
> "Hello, this is [Name] from [Clinic]. We noticed you're due for your [annual physical/diabetes check/mammogram]. We'd like to help you schedule an appointment. Do you have a few minutes?"

- Offer scheduling assistance
- Answer questions about the visit

#### Step 4: Document All Attempts
- Date and time of contact
- Method (phone, letter, portal message)
- Outcome (scheduled, declined, no answer, wrong number)
- Follow-up needed
- Update recall status

#### Step 5: Follow-Up Protocol
- First attempt: Phone call
- Second attempt (1 week later): Phone call + portal message
- Third attempt (2 weeks later): Mailed letter
- After 3 attempts: Document and close outreach cycle
- Repeat cycle per clinic policy (quarterly, annually)

## Insurance & Eligibility

### 9. Insurance Eligibility Verification
ðŸ” *Timing-Based Task*

Proper eligibility verification prevents claim denials and patient surprises.

#### When to Run Eligibility

| Scenario | When to Verify |
|----------|---------------|
| New patient | At registration |
| Existing patient â€” new insurance | When insurance changes |
| Existing patient â€” same insurance | Monthly (or per policy) |
| Eligibility returned unclear | Day prior to visit |
| High-dollar procedure scheduled | 48-72 hours prior |

#### Step 1: Access Eligibility Tool
- Open practice management system
- Navigate to eligibility verification
- Enter patient's insurance information
- Submit real-time eligibility request

#### Step 2: Review Response
- **Active Coverage:** Confirm effective date covers appointment date
- **Patient Responsibility:**
  - Copay amount
  - Deductible remaining
  - Coinsurance percentage
  - Out-of-pocket maximum status
- **Plan Details:**
  - In-network status
  - Referral/authorization requirements
  - PCP assignment (if HMO)

#### Step 3: Address Issues
If eligibility doesn't return clear:
- Double-check member ID and DOB entered correctly
- Try alternate payer ID if available
- Call payer directly if electronic check fails
- Run again day prior to visit
- Document all attempts

#### Step 4: Document Verification
- Record verification date
- Note copay/coinsurance amounts
- Flag any authorization requirements
- Document who verified and method used
- Set reminder if re-verification needed

#### Step 5: Communicate with Patient (If Issues)
If coverage not active or not accepted:
- Contact patient before appointment
- Explain: "We were unable to verify your insurance coverage"
- Discuss options: Updated insurance info, self-pay, reschedule
- Document conversation

> **Key Point:** Never assume coverage. Verify every time, even for established patients.

## Forms & Compliance

### 10. ROI, Consent Forms & NPP
ðŸ“ *At Registration & Annually*

Proper documentation protects patients, staff, and the organization.

#### Understanding the Forms

| Form | What It Is | When Required |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **NPP** (Notice of Privacy Practices) | Explains how patient health info is used/shared | New patients; when updated |
| **Consent for Treatment** | Authorizes clinic to provide care | New patients; annually |
| **Financial Responsibility** | Patient agrees to pay for services | New patients; when policy changes |
| **HIPAA Acknowledgment** | Confirms patient received NPP | New patients; annually |
| **ROI** (Release of Information) | Authorizes sharing records with specific parties | As needed per request |

#### Step 1: New Patient Form Collection
Required at first visit:
- [ ] Consent for Treatment â€” signed and dated
- [ ] HIPAA/NPP Acknowledgment â€” signed and dated
- [ ] Financial Responsibility Agreement â€” signed and dated
- [ ] Patient Demographics Form â€” completed
- [ ] Medical History Questionnaire â€” completed
- [ ] Current Medications List â€” completed
- [ ] Pharmacy Information â€” provided

#### Step 2: Annual Updates
Check annually (typically at first visit of the year):
- HIPAA acknowledgment current?
- Financial policy re-signed if updated?
- Demographics verified and updated?
- Insurance cards re-scanned?

#### Step 3: Processing Release of Information (ROI)
When patient requests records be sent elsewhere:

1. **Obtain Written Authorization**
   - Use clinic's official ROI form
   - Must include:
     - Patient name and DOB
     - What information to release
     - Who to release to (name, address, fax)
     - Purpose of release
     - Expiration date
     - Patient signature and date

2. **Verify Authorization**
   - Is signature authentic?
   - Is request within date range?
   - Is information requested appropriate?
   - Any restrictions noted?

3. **Process Request**
   - Pull requested records
   - Review for completeness
   - Send via secure method (fax, mail, portal)
   - Document: Date sent, what sent, where sent, by whom

4. **Exceptions â€” No ROI Needed**
   - Treatment purposes (referrals between providers)
   - Payment purposes (sending to insurance)
   - Healthcare operations
   - Patient requesting own records
   - Legal requirements (subpoenas, public health)

> âš ï¸ **HIPAA Alert:** When in doubt, get written authorization. Never release records to family members or others without patient consent (unless legally authorized).

### 11. New Patient Check-In
ðŸ‘‹ *Applies to: New Patients Only*

Comprehensive check-in sets the tone for the patient relationship.

#### Step 1: Greet Warmly
- Make eye contact and smile
- Stand if possible
- "Good morning/afternoon! Welcome to [Clinic Name]"
- "Is this your first visit with us?"
- Offer seat at check-in desk or private area

#### Step 2: Verify Identity
- "May I see your photo ID and insurance card?"
- Check photo matches patient
- Verify name spelling matches registration
- Confirm date of birth verbally
- Check insurance card matches system
- If discrepancy: "Has anything changed since you registered?"

#### Step 3: Scan Documents
- Scan/copy front and back of ID
- Scan front and back of insurance card(s)
- Upload to patient account
- Return originals immediately
- Note: "ID and insurance scanned [date]"

#### Step 4: Confirm Insurance Eligibility
- If verified 48-72 hours ago, quick confirmation check
- Verify coverage still active today
- Confirm no insurance changes since verification
- Flag any issues for billing team

#### Step 5: Collect & Review Forms
- "Were you able to complete the forms we sent?"
- If electronic: Pull up and verify completion
- If not complete: Provide paper forms with clipboard
- Required:
  - [ ] Medical history
  - [ ] Current medications
  - [ ] Allergies
  - [ ] HIPAA consent
  - [ ] Financial policy
  - [ ] Consent for treatment
- Check for signatures and dates on all forms
- Ask patient to complete missing sections

#### Step 6: Update & Confirm Information
- Verify address current
- Confirm phone numbers
- Verify email
- Confirm emergency contact
- Note preferred language and communication method
- Update changes in system immediately

#### Step 7: Collect Copay
- State clearly: "Your copay today is $[amount]"
- "This is required by your insurance at time of service"
- Accept: Cash, check, or card
- Process payment immediately
- Apply to today's date of service
- Provide receipt
- Thank patient

#### Step 8: Set Up Patient Portal
- "Have you activated your patient portal account?"
- If no: Send activation email or provide instructions
- Explain features: Results, messaging, appointments, refills
- Offer quick demonstration
- Provide support phone number

#### Step 9: Explain Key Policies
- Office hours and after-hours contact
- Prescription refill process
- Cancellation/no-show policy
- Portal use for non-urgent questions
- Payment policy for balances

#### Step 10: Orient to Facility
- Point out restroom
- Indicate waiting area
- "Please have a seat; we'll call you shortly"
- Mention estimated wait time if known
- Point out water/refreshments if available

#### Step 11: Notify Clinical Staff
- Update patient status to "Checked In"
- Alert clinical staff new patient is ready
- Note any special circumstances
- Flag urgent concerns or incomplete forms
- Document check-in time

ðŸš¨ *Applies to: Both New & Existing Patients*

Balancing urgency with proper documentation.

- Greet patient promptly

- Phone number
- Insurance card (scan)

- Allergies
- Current medications (if relevant to complaint)

#### Step 5: Quick Eligibility Check
- Run real-time eligibility
- Determine copay
- If unable to verify: Inform patient they may be responsible
- Document attempt

#### Step 6: Collect Copay
- Collect if known
- If unknown: "We'll determine your responsibility and collect at checkout"
- Document

#### Step 7: Alert Clinical Team
- Notify immediately of walk-in
- Provide: Patient name, chief complaint, urgency level
- Communicate any clinical concerns
- Update status to "Checked In â€” Walk-In"

#### Step 8: Communicate Wait Time
- Be honest: "The wait may be [X] minutes as we work you in"
- Offer updates if delay extends
- Thank patient for patience

#### Step 9: Complete Documentation (Post-Visit If Needed)
- Finish demographics
- Collect remaining forms
- Scan ID and insurance if not done
- Complete any skipped steps

## Throughout the Day Operations

### 14. Balancing Phones, Messages & Walk-Ins
ðŸŽ¯ *Continuous Throughout Day*

Managing multiple demands while maintaining service quality.

#### Step 1: Prioritize by Urgency

| Level | Examples | Response Time |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **1 â€” Immediate** | Patient in distress, emergency calls, provider STAT requests | Now |
| **2 â€” High** | Patient at window, checkout, time-sensitive calls | Within 2-3 min |
| **3 â€” Standard** | General calls, message returns, scheduling requests | Within 30 min |
| **4 â€” Low** | Filing, data entry, non-urgent emails | As time permits |

> **Key Rule:** Address the person in front of you before starting new tasks (unless true emergency).

#### Step 2: Managing Walk-Ins
- Acknowledge immediately with eye contact and smile
- If busy: "I'll be with you in just one moment"
- Finish current task or reach stopping point (2-3 min max)
- "How can I help you today?"
- Determine urgency and process accordingly

#### Step 3: Answering Phones
- Answer within 3 rings when possible
- If with patient: "Excuse me one moment"
- Answer: "[Clinic Name], this is [Name], how may I help you?"
- For brief questions: Handle immediately
- For complex issues: Take message or schedule callback
- If genuinely unable to answer: Let voicemail capture, return within policy timeframe

#### Step 4: When Interrupted by Phone
- "Excuse me, I need to answer this quickly"
- Answer and assess urgency immediately
- If not urgent: "May I get your name and call you back in 5 minutes?"
- If urgent: Handle call, apologize to waiting patient
- Return to patient, summarize where you left off
- Complete interaction before making callbacks

#### Step 5: Processing Messages
- Check queue at designated times (or continuously if light)
- Sort by urgency and date received
- Respond to time-sensitive first
- Prescription refills: Forward to clinical team immediately
- Appointment requests: Process during lulls
- Billing questions: Respond or route to billing
- Document all responses

#### Step 6: Team Coordination
- Designate roles if multiple staff (one check-in, one phones)
- Batch similar tasks during quieter moments
- Keep frequently needed info accessible
- Use templates for common responses
- Communicate when overwhelmed
- Ask for help when needed

#### Step 7: Maintaining Professionalism
- Deep breath before greeting next person
- Never show frustration to patients
- Speak clearly and calmly when rushed
- Okay to say: "We're experiencing high volume today"
- Set realistic expectations for wait times
- Remember: Patients don't see your other demands

### 16. Handling STAT Results

### 17. Managing Waitlists & Bumplists
ðŸ“ *Ongoing Task*

Optimizing schedule utilization through effective waitlist management.

#### Step 1: Create & Maintain Waitlist
When patient requests earlier appointment than available:
- Record patient name and contact number
- Note current appointment date/time
- Document preferred days/times
- Note provider preference
- Ask: "How much notice do you need?"
- Document reason for visit / urgency
- Update list daily; remove patients who no longer need earlier slots

#### Step 2: Prioritize Waitlist

| Priority | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| 1 | Urgent medical needs |
| 2 | Established patients (per policy) |
| 3 | Time on waitlist (first-come within priority) |
| 4 | Short-notice availability |

Flag patients with special needs or accommodations

#### Step 3: When a Slot Opens
- Review waitlist immediately
- Match appointment type and duration
- Verify provider matches patient need
- Contact highest priority patient first
- "We have an opening for [date/time]. Can you make it?"
- Give 5-10 minutes to respond before moving to next

#### Step 4: Confirm Waitlist Appointment
- If accepted: Book immediately
- Cancel/move original later appointment
- Send confirmation
- Remove from waitlist
- Update schedule, notify clinical staff
- Document who was offered and outcome

#### Step 5: Same-Day Add-On Requests
- Ask about reason and urgency
- Determine if truly urgent or can wait
- Check with clinical staff: "Do we have capacity?"
- Review for gaps: Lunch, end of day, cancellations
- Be honest about wait times

#### Step 6: Assess Appropriateness
Consider:
- Is this a true medical urgency?
- Can it be handled via phone triage or telehealth?
- Is urgent care/ER more appropriate?
- Is this an established patient?
- Will adding significantly delay others?

#### Step 7: Approve or Decline
- If approved: Add to schedule, inform clinical team, set wait expectations
- If declined: "We're fully booked, but I have [next available]"
- Offer waitlist or next appointment
- Provide alternatives if urgent (urgent care, ER)
- Offer nurse callback for triage if appropriate
- Document decision and reason

### 18. Handling No-Shows & Late Arrivals
â° *As Situations Arise*

Managing schedule disruptions while maintaining patient relationships.

#### Step 1: Monitor Schedule
- Review daily schedule at shift start
- Note patients with history of no-shows/late arrivals
- Confirm all appointments were reminded
- Flag new patients and high-priority appointments

#### Step 2: Late Arrival â€” Within Tolerance
(Typically 10-15 minutes per policy)
- Greet professionally without frustration
- Check in promptly
- Inform clinical staff immediately
- Ask clinical team if patient can still be seen
- If seen: Explain appointment time may be reduced
- Document actual arrival time

#### Step 3: Late Arrival â€” Beyond Tolerance
- Greet warmly but explain policy
- "Our policy requires arrival [X] minutes before appointment time"
- Apologize that provider cannot see them today
- Offer to reschedule at next available time
- Remind about importance of timely arrival
- Document late arrival and rescheduling

#### Step 4: No-Show Protocol
- Wait [X] minutes past appointment time (per policy, typically 10-15)
- Attempt phone contact
- Leave voicemail: "We missed you today for your [time] appointment with [Provider]. Please call us at [number] to reschedule."
- Mark as "No-Show" in system
- Send portal message or text if available
- Document in patient chart

#### Step 5: Apply No-Show Policy
- Document no-show count in record
- First no-show: Reschedule with reminder about policy
- Second no-show: Inform patient of consequences
- Third no-show (or per policy): May require deposit for future appointments
- Follow progressive policy (warnings, fees, potential dismissal)
- Document all policy discussions

#### Step 6: Fill Cancelled/No-Show Slots
- Check waitlist immediately
- Contact waitlist patients
- Offer same-day or next available
- Prioritize urgent needs and established patients
- Update schedule and remove from waitlist if accepted
- Keep backup list of short-notice patients

#### Step 7: Document & Report
- Track no-show rates by provider, day, time
- Report patterns to office manager
- Identify chronic no-show patients
- Suggest improvements to reduce no-shows
- Keep records for billing/dismissal documentation

### Emergency Quick Reference

**If patient appears to be having a medical emergency:**
1. Stay calm
2. Alert clinical staff immediately
3. Call 911 if directed
4. Stay with patient until help arrives
5. Do not move patient unless necessary for safety
6. Document incident after resolved

**If you suspect credit card fraud or tampering:**
1. Do not use terminal
2. Alert supervisor immediately
3. Do not touch or alter terminal
4. Document what you observed

**If patient becomes aggressive or threatening:**

2. Do not argue or escalate
3. Create distance if possible
4. Alert supervisor/security
5. Call 911 if immediate danger
6. Document incident after resolved
